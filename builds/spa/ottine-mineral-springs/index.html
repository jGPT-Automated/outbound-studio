The History

From oil derrick to polio ward to wellness sanctuary.

1909
The wildcatters strike water.

A drilling crew including Howard Hughes Sr. taps into the Carrizo-Wilcox aquifer searching for oil. What surfaces instead is 106°F mineral water — and the beginning of Ottine.

1930s
Gonzales Warm Springs Foundation.

The site becomes the only desegregated polio care facility in the United States. Children of every background receive hydrotherapy in the same waters that draw guests today.

1947
A full campus.

Dorms, pools, a school, an auditorium, and a chapel serve over 400 children annually — a community built around the healing waters.

Post-Salk
Mission expands.

After the polio vaccine, the foundation shifts to serve children with all physical disabilities — continuing decades of inclusive care.

Today
Reopened, restored.

Under new stewardship, Ottine returns as a wellness destination — the same water, the same ground, a new chapter.

"The water doesn't change. The story does."
